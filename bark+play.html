bark+play;

Bark+Play helps owners socialize their dogs by finding play dates for them. It also encourages people to build stronger interpersonal relationships with others in their community.

This is a personal project inspired by my experience with socializing my dog and my interactions with members of my community.

Role: As a UX Designer, I conducted interviews to understand the problem and users, did some competitive analysis, developed personas, brainstormed potential solutions, mapped the task flow, developed a brand, and created UI mockups.

the initial problem

Socializing a dog is an important responsibility as an owner. A lack of socialization may lead dogs to become defensive at the sight of other dogs. Like us, dogs learn through experience and allowing them to experience the world is the best way to teach them how to behave properly. How, though, do we help teach dogs who aren’t constantly exposed to other dogs to behave properly?

socializing is hard

I conducted qualitative interviews with five dog owners (ages 18-24) who enjoy walking their dogs or taking them to parks. I wanted to understand how they go about socializing their dogs and what the challenges are.

I asked them questions about their experiences with walking their dogs, meeting other dog owners, and dealing with socialization issues.

key insights and opportunities

  • Because of time, owners tug on their dog's leash to keep them walking rather than stopping to socialize

How might we... encourage owners to let their dogs interact with others?

  • Dogs naturally want to interact with other dogs because they're curious, but we as owners sometimes prevent them from doing so

How might we... create/facilitate time for owners to let their dogs interact with other dogs?

  • Sometimes, dogs may not come across other dogs on their regular walks which is a lost opportunity

How might we... assist owners to form connections with other owners within a neighborhood?

  • It can feel odd sometimes to strike up conversations with others during a walk if there is no common ground

the new problem

After the interviews, I felt that the socialization issue was larger in that it wasn’t just something that dogs had to improve on, but also us as members of a community. I believe that we are all attached to our phones — myself included — because most of us use them to access important information like our bank accounts, emails, and social media. In relying on our phones to connect to our world, we become detached from our physical community. I understand why it’s hard for everyone to disconnect — I empathize because I also struggle — but our phone doesn’t have to be a problem. It can instead be the medium for solving the socialization issue present in our community and subsequently, our dogs.

what's out there?

I brainstormed possible ways to help people form connections within their community with the goal of also helping dogs socialize. I didn't limit myself. At this point, any idea was a good one.

When reviewing what I had come up with, I realized that some ideas were costly — a public portal that works in conjuction with the city's animal shelter database of registered dogs where registered owners can request to meet up with others... pretty messy and pretty costly. Also, the problem with this potential solution is that it impinges on the public's choice to participate.

Since nearly everyone uses their phone for daily tasks, I decided to design an app that allows owners to voluntarily sign up to meet with other owners in the area. Before starting, I did some research to see if there were any existing apps and to take note of what I thought they were doing right and what they could improve:

Twindog

main features

  1. Swipe left to pass, right to match/request to meet up
  2. Profile — picture of dog and owner; dog's age, name, and breed
  3. Matches are suggested based on location

notable features

  1. Feedback is simple and informational — if swipe left (pass) image of dog moves left, if swipe right (accept) and it's a match, the options to send message or keep playing (find someone else) pops up
  2. Very minimal design helps users focus on the dogs and people, not the app
  3. Less keyboard, more touch approach — the conversation happens away from the screen

possible issues

  1. App is easily confused with a dating app because it's marketed that way, someone who only wants to socialize with others may not want to use it
  2. No way for users to look up dogs they've met or liked
  3. No way to filter dogs based on age, proximity, and such

Meet My Dog

main features

  1. Activity: user can check-in at a location to notify friends where they are and invite them to join
  2. Proximity: shows list of dogs in the area
  3. Chats: private messages between users
  4. Friends: add users as friends for a closer network
  5. Account: shows owner and dog info (dog name, location, sex, breed)

notable features

  1. Users are told how close other dogs are to their current location and provides the name and picture of the dog
  2. Users can search for locations to check in to
  3. Private messaging supports image attachment

possible issues

  1. Much of the design and function is taken from Facebook
  2. GPS continually runs in the background which may drain battery life very quickly
  3. UI appears a bit complex — users may focus more on using the app than finding the perfect friend for their dog

user flow

key screens

nearby dogs

The app uses the phone's GPS feature to determine where a user is and to share that with nearby dog owners. Safety is a priority, so this feature is only in effect if a user gives permission — it can be changed in the app's settings so that it doesn't use GPS at all or so that it only uses it when the app has been opened. If GPS isn't used, then only the user's city is displayed on the list of nearby dogs.

private messaging

Sometimes a user may want to ask more questions about someone's dog before scheduling a play date, or perhaps a user may want to suggest a meet up location. Maybe they just want to share pictures from their last play date. For many reasons, a private messaging feature allows users to get in touch and keep in touch.

user profiles

We've all grown accustomed to having our own profiles online for the purpose of sharing our basic information with friends/followers. Bark + Play's profiles help users learn about each other and about their dogs. By giving users a profile, they will feel more comfortable with requesting a play date because they will have learned a little bit more about the person/dog they will be meeting with.

finding friends

If the map view of nearby dogs isn't helpful — as in, perhaps no one in the area is using GPS to show where they are — then users may look through a list of dogs living in the city. If users have their GPS enabled within the app, then the list will display the distance between them and another dog. Of course, the other user must also have their GPS enabled in order for the distance to display. Aside from this list, users may also view their friends list and request to meet up with one of them.

adding friends

Building a network within a community is something that Bark + Play also aims to achieve. If play dates are successful, then users are encouraged to add someone to their friends list. By adding this feature, I hope to encourage users to form connections with people in their neighborhood and push them to start conversations beyond the screen. Subsequently, through these new formed friendships, the user's dogs will have regular play dates and new friends, which translates to good social skills.

next steps

Regarding the design process, my next step would be to present my design to users so that I may determine what can be improved. Only through testing would I be able to learn how well my solution satisfies the needs of Bark + Play's potential users.

I know that there is always room for improvement, so after testing with users and making necessary changes, I would work to develop a prototype to test for screen flow and usability issues. Since I'm not a developer, I would want to collaborate with a development team which would be a great opportunity to use my product management skills.

reflection

Through this project, I learned how to understand users through empathy and through creating a product that facilitates behavioral change and delivers value to its users. I also practiced weaving digital and physical experiences into a product that emphasizes building interpersonal relationships in a real life setting. Bark + Play's purpose is to encourage users to meet members of their community and share their love for dogs while helping their dogs learn to socialize through play dates. I would hope that these interactions would generate awareness of diversity and connection within a neighborhood.

I'm always looking to improve as a designer and a big part of this process is learning what my strengths and weaknesses are. I'm not a visual designer, but this project helped me develop my UI design skills concerning color, layout, and typography. I learned that I'm a minimalist when it comes to design because to me, a simple UI can help users focus more on the actual experience and content.
