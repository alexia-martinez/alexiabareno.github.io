ageless link;

Ageless Link, in partnership with Ageless Alliance, is a nonprofit web solution that bridges volunteers with isolated elders in Orange County as a prevention for elder abuse, neglect, and exploitation. This project came from my senior design course at UC Irvine.

Our solution pairs volunteers with elders, allows volunteers to record their service hours, and gives the administrator access to a dashboard that provides an overview of volunteer activity which includes logged service hours, new volunteer applications, and volunteer-elder matches.

Role: As a UX Designer, I conducted user research, produced sketches and wireframes using Axure, and helped with web development (Bootstrap, HTML, and CSS.)

what's the problem?

The founder of Ageless Alliance initially asked for a creative software solution (computer and phone use) that collects information from elders and volunteers. I felt that we did not have enough information to understand what was being asked of us — was there a current method of collecting information that didn't work? What about it wasn't working? A peer and I decided to meet with the founder and ask her questions to learn more about her organization and to identify the problem(s).

Our research on Ageless Alliance began with interviewing the founder and the social media manager/admin. They explained the mission of the organization and its involvement with the elderly community. We learned that the organization's most valuable resource was its volunteer group which devotes time to assist elders with everyday tasks and most importantly, keeps the elders company by visiting their homes every so often. We asked our clients about the challenges they were facing in managing the organization and discovered that managing volunteer groups and member information was the problem. We learned that:

  1. It was hard to keep track of all elder-volunteer matches as they were manually done
  2. There was no visibility into volunteer activity and the volunteers did not always log in hours so the organization assumed they didn't visit the elder
  3. The volunteers only knew who and when to visit after receiving a call from a lead member
  4. The volunteers weren't always able to drive out to visit the elder which made some elders feel sad

We understood that the founder wanted an improved way to match elders with volunteers (these matches are the 'information' she was referring to in her initial request), but that she really needed a better way to direct and track volunteer activity. The problem then became clear: How can we create and track volunteer-elder matches?

After discussion, my team and our partner agreed that a website (portal) would benefit the organization greatly. Volunteers would use it to log in their hours and to see who they had been matched with. They would also be able to log their hours on the go via their mobile device. The administrator(s) would access a dashboard of volunteer activity. Our clients also asked to make available information about the organization and about how to become a volunteer.

Prior to beginning the design of our website, I did a competitive analysis of three similar sites to become aware of what other sites had content-wise and to see what worked and what didn't.

meeting our users

Now that we had a clear view of our problem, the next step was to gain a clear understanding of our users: the volunteers and the administrator(s). We did not include elders for two reasons: (a) although many elders are technologically savvy, not all are and we did not want to risk the possibility of excluding some of our elders from being a part of this program, and (b) all relevant elder information is already held by the organization, so there's no need for an elder to have an account.

I helped conduct interviews with 5 volunteers to learn more about the volunteer role and to identify any pain points that might be present in the volunteer routine. Our volunteers were men and women of ages 40-54 who felt comfortable using the internet, computers, and mobile devices.

key insights and opportunities

VOLUNTEERS

How might we... make it easy for volunteers to log in hours?

  • Volunteers have other responsibilities and driving to the office to log hours can be inconvenient

How might we... let volunteers know who to contact for assistance?

  • Depending on the situation, volunteers may need to call or message. Establishing a 'help' center can direct volunteers to the right people

How might we... inform volunteers of their duties as a volunteer?

  • Volunteers are given a short guide to what's expected of them, but establishing a comprehensive online guide may help both to inform the volunteer and to reduce paper use

administrator

key insights and opportunities

ADMINISTRATOR

How might we... assist the admin in matching and tracking volunteer-elder pairs?

  • If we can automatically pair volunteers with elders, we can help the administrator devote her time to other important tasks

How might we... simplify the process of processing volunteer applications?

  • Applying online would rid of paper applications and make it easier to keep track of which ones need to be processed

How might we... give the admin visibility into volunteer activity?

  • If we can notify the admin about new applicants, hibernating volunteers, active volunteers, etc. then we can make it less stressful for leadership to run the organization

reflection

The biggest challenge was identifying the problem because our client had a difficult time explaining what she needed to be done. This project taught me that research is imperative to projects like this because in taking the time to investigate the problem, we identified other underlying issues that could have otherwise gone unnoticed.

This course did not have any 'rules' to follow and there were no specific assignments to turn in except for documentation. Apporaching this project with the design thinking method helped us stay on track and focused on our users. If we only relied on the information our clients shared with us without checking in with our users, we most likely would have delivered a useless product.
